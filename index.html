Flexbox Froggies history

Flexbox Froggy is an educational game created to teach web developers the intricacies of the CSS Flexbox layout system in a fun and interactive way. The game was developed by Codepip (formerly known as Thomas Park) in 2016 as a creative way to help learners grasp the often confusing concepts of Flexbox, one of the most powerful layout techniques in modern web development.

Stories of Flexbox Froggy

The Great River Rescue

One day, Flexbox Froggy was hopping along the shores of Code River when he noticed a group of distressed tadpoles caught in the rapid current, unable to escape. With his mastery of alignment and spacing, Flexbox Froggy swiftly constructed a flexible, invisible bridge across the water, perfectly spaced for the tadpoles to hop along to safety. His quick thinking and ability to align the world to his will saved the young tadpoles, earning him the title of "The Guardian of Flow," admired by frogs and coders alike.
